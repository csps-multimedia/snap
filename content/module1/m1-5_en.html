Supporting Legislations

Access to Information Act

Access to Information Act – gives Canadian citizens the right to access information in federal government records. It relates closely to security because it provides the legislative basis for the release, or exemptions from release, of government information.

Privacy Act

Privacy Act– protects the privacy of individuals by setting requirements for collection, use, retention or disposal of personal information in the federal government. Government officials are required to protect, from unauthorized use or disclosure, all personal information the Government collects. Security plays an essential role in the protection of personal information from unauthorized disclosure or use.

Canada Labour Code

Canada Labour Code – Part II of the Canada Labour Code on occupational health and safety provides guidance to the federal government to prevent accidents and injury. This includes measures to protect employees (e.g. prevention of violence in the workplace).

Emergency Management Act

Emergency Management Act– sets out how to prepare, prevent, mitigate and recover when faced with national emergency situations and disruption of critical services that impact the health, safety, security and economic well-being of Canadians. Security plays an essential role in the establishment of business continuity plans.
