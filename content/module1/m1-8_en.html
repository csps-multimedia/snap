Security Screening and Need to Know

A fundamental principle in government security is that only authorized individuals who have the proper security screening and a need to know are given access to sensitive information.

Security Screening

The purpose of security screening is to ensure that those who have access to government information, assets and services are trustworthy, reliable and loyal.

The security screening process will examine the honesty, trustworthiness, reliability, and loyalty to Canada of individuals.

Security screening is a condition of employment in the government. You cannot be appointed to a position without the required reliability status or security clearance.

More details are provided in Module 4.

Need to Know

No employee is entitled to have access to all sensitive information solely by virtue of rank or appointment.

Similarly, your level of security screening does not automatically give you access to all documents in the associated security category. You should only have access to information that you need to do your job. This is known as the “need to know” principle.
