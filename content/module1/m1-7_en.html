Confidentiality, Privacy, Integrity and Availability of Information

A fundamental objective of security is to ensure the confidentiality, privacy, integrity, and availability of information. This is essential to government decision making and the delivery of services.

If your organization’s information is compromised, the impact could jeopardize your department’s reputation and its ability to achieve its mandate.

Effective information security requires a systematic approach to identifying and categorizing information according to the impact of a compromise, to assess the risks, and to implement appropriate security safeguards.

More detailed information on the confidentiality, privacy, integrity and availability of information is provided in Module 6.

Confidentiality, Privacy, Integrity, and Availability

  • Confidentiality: Refers to the need to protect information from unauthorized access or disclosure.
  • Privacy: Refers to the need to protect personal information collected by the government from unauthorized access or disclosure.
  • Integrity: Primarily concerned with the need to protect information from unauthorized modification, which can include protecting the authenticity of information.

  • Availability: Concerned with protection of information from unauthorized destruction or removal or disruption of services.
