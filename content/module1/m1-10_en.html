Roles and Responsibilities for Management of Security

This section summarizes the roles and responsibilities of management for security within your department.

Everyone has a role to play in your department’s security program. Security is most effective when it is systematically woven into the business, programs and culture of a department and the public service as a whole.

Deputy Head

Is accountable for security in your department and for establishing effective security management and governance.

Is responsible for establishing practices for the protection and management of personal information.

Chief Security Officer (CSO)

Is responsible for overseeing the establishment and maintenance of department-wide processes related to their organization’s security program.

Senior Management

Must address security requirements in the department's strategic direction, plans and priorities, and resources.

Program and Service Delivery Managers

Are responsible for ensuring an appropriate level of security for their programs and services based on sound security risk-management practices.

Are also responsible for informing employees of the legal and administrative consequences of any inappropriate or unauthorized access to; or use, disclosure, modification, retention and disposition of personal information related to a particular program or activity.

Managers at all levels

Are responsible for ensuring the protection of employees and ensuring employees apply effective security practices in day-to-day operations.

Employees

Are responsible for:

  • safeguarding information and assets under their control, whether working on or off site
  • reporting security incidents through the appropriate channels and taking direction from the departmental security office
  • maintaining awareness of security concerns and issues to ensure their actions do not compromise departmental security
  • protecting the privacy of personal information under their control
