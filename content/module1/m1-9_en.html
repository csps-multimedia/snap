Values and Ethics in the Public Service

As a public servant, you are required to abide by established public service values, ethics and expected behaviours in everything you do. Ethical behaviour is a foundation of all public sector duties, including security.

Everyone needs to be security conscious. Appropriate ethical and professional behaviour is fundamental to security. For example:

Respect For Democracy

Public servants shall uphold the Canadian parliamentary democracy and its institutions by respecting the rule of law and carrying out their duties in accordance with the Policy on Government Security and all supporting legislation, policies and directives in a non-partisan and impartial manner.

Respect For People

Public servants shall respect human dignity and the value of every person by:

  • Treating every person with respect and fairness.
  • Valuing diversity and the benefit of combining the unique qualities and strengths inherent in a diverse workforce.
  • Helping to create and maintain safe and healthy workplaces that are free from harassment and discrimination.
  • Working together in a spirit of openness, honesty and transparency that encourages engagement, collaboration and respectful communication.
Integrity

You are required to perform your duties with integrity and in a manner that will bear the closest public scrutiny. When privy to sensitive information, you demonstrate integrity by treating the information carefully and respectfully to protect its sensitivity and to ensure it is not used for personal gain. You also have an obligation to protect personal information from unauthorized access or disclosure.

Stewardship

You are entrusted to use and care for public resources responsibly, including information. Sensitive information that you receive through your employment must not be divulged to anyone other than persons who are authorized to receive the information. Caution and discretion in handling sensitive information in public spaces extends to disclosure inside and outside government. You have an obligation to manage personal information responsibly.

Note: If your actions are found to contradict your responsibilities as specified in the Values and Ethics Code for the Public Sector or if you commit any wrongdoing, you could be subject to an administrative investigation and place your job at risk. Since your security screening is a condition of employment, you could also lose your position if you fail to maintain your reliability status or security clearance.
