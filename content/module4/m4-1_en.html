What is Physical Security?

Physical security refers to the use of safeguards that reduce the risk of violence to employees, protect against unauthorized access, detect attempted or actual unauthorized access and activate an effective response.

Physical security involves the use of multiple layers of interdependent systems such as video surveillance, security guards, protective barriers, cabinets and locks, access control protocols, and intrusion alarms.

The Directive on Security Management stipulates that “Access to government assets and facilities is limited to authorized individuals who have been security screened at the appropriate level and who have an express need for access.”

Physical security safeguards are based on:

  • Securing facilities using a hierarchy of clearly discernable “zones”
  • Restricting access to control areas
  • Increasing security during emergencies and increased threat situations
