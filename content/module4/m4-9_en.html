Access Control Methods

There are a variety of methods to control access, including:

  • administrative procedures such as having visitors sign in and out
  • having employees show identification cards to security personnel
  • having visitors contact an employee to escort them into the facility
  • electronic access control (e.g. card access, PIN access or biometric access control)
  • use of physical or mechanical barriers to resist forced entry or to control access (e.g. locked doors, turnstiles and gates)
  • administrative procedures to signing into an office area or require someone to sign out or record files
  • if available use specially constructed “secure rooms” to store protected and classified information on open shelves. A secure room is in essence a "security container" and subject to the same zoning requirements
  • use approved “sensitive discussion areas”, which are areas that are specially designed and managed to prevent the overhearing of sensitive discussions
