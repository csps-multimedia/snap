Piggybacking

Piggybacking refers to when an individual tags along or follows an authorized employee through a security checkpoint or doorway without being processed through the system. The act may be legal or illegal, authorized or unauthorized, depending on the circumstances.

This method of entry is unacceptable and employees must be vigilant in following proper security procedures for control of access.

Remember!

Despite all of the physical security measures in place, employees are the best defense.

If you see something, SAY SOMETHING!

All individuals without the proper security screening (i.e. contractors, visitors, etc.) must be escorted at all times. The employee who signed-in the individual is responsible for him/her while on the premises.

Also, keep in mind that all employees will have a similar-looking access cards, but not every access card gives authorized access to the same areas or zones.
