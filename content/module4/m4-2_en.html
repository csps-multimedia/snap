Identification and Government Access Cards

Identification cards (ID) and access cards are different. Although they may appear similar, they serve different purposes.

Please note that some departments and organizations incorporate the identification card/access card requirements on one credential.

Select each one of the cards to learn more.

Identification Card:

All government employees are issued an identification card that shows the individual's name, the name of the issuing department/organization, expiry date, a unique number, and a colour photograph. A signature is also recommended.

Confirms that the individual is an employee. It is issued when the employee has been hired and has the appropriate security screening required for the position. You are responsible for any activities occurring with your card(s).

Should be carried at all times while on government premises. They need to be renewed every 5 years or when major identification (physical) features have changed (e.g. beard, moustache, hair style/colour, weight gain/loss, etc.). If you think you need a new card, contact your departmental security division to confirm.

Some departments may issue identification cards to persons not fitting the traditional definition of an employee (i.e. a student, volunteer or contract worker) and may use an “affiliation” field to identify the status of the individual.

Access Card:

Is used to quickly and effectively determine whether an individual is authorized to access a facility, complex or zone. An access card confirms that the individual has been properly processed through the access control procedures.

Indicates access authorization only. Control staff may be used at an entry point to verify that persons entering the restricted areas are wearing an appropriate access card. The card may also be combined with an electronic access control system to permit access.

Must be worn at all times by everyone while on government premises, including affiliates, employees, contractors and visitors.

An employee’s access card should provide a photo of the bearer. A temporary access card should be worn by all visitors (including non-authorized employees, contractors, service personnel) that clearly identify them as non-employees. The visitor card should stipulate whether or not an escort is required.
