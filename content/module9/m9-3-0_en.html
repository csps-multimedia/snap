Preparing Employees for Emergencies

While TBS has decision-making authority for policy matters related to occupational health and safety (OHS) in the federal public service, departments and agencies are responsible for the development and establishment of a safety culture at work.

Good health and safety practices contribute to the objective of supporting the development of a high-performing workforce that provides good governance and service to Canadians.

It is every employerâ€™s responsibility to provide safe occupancy of the workplace and to prepare for emergencies and evacuations. TBS has developed a new Directive on Building Emergency and Evacuation Teams (BEET), which includes all types of hazards and events that could trigger emergency procedures. The directive:

  • addresses all potential hazards
  • clarifies responsibilities and accountabilities in multi-tenants buildings
  • addresses the information flow between the emergency and evacuation team, the workplace health and safety committees and other stakeholders
  • ensures a consistent government-wide approach in preparing for emergencies and evacuations
  • emphasizes the importance of training and exercise activities by requiring, at minimum, one annual evacuation drill and one non-evacuation exercise (lockdown or shelter-in-place) for all federal employees
     

TBS has also developed the Preparing for Emergencies and Evacuation of Buildings: A guide for federal departments, which provides useful information and tools to help organizations meet the objectives and requirements of the policy instruments.
