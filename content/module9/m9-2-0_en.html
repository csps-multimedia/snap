Violence in the Workplace

Protecting employees from violence in the workplace is of critical importance and is the employer’s responsibility under the Canada Labour Code, Part II. Although we do not normally go to work expecting violence to occur, it can happen and can have a serious impact on those affected, their families, and the workplace.

The Policy on Government Security makes references to the term “workplace violence.” Part XX of the Occupational Health and Safety Regulations defines workplace violence as: “any action, conduct, threat or gesture by a person towards an employee in their workplace that can reasonably be expected to cause harm, injury or illness to that employee.”
