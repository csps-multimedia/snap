Roles and Responsibilities

It is the employer’s responsibility under the Canada Labour Code, Part II, to ensure that the health and safety of every person employed by the employer is protected at work. The responsibility also applies to every person granted access to the workplace.

Employees' rights
  • The Right to know and be made aware of every known or foreseeable health and safety hazard in the workplace (e.g. by attending health information/training sessions)
  • The Right to participate in keeping the workplace healthy and safe (e.g. volunteering with health and safety committees)
  • The Right to refuse to work when there is a danger
Employee responsibilities
  • To take all reasonable and necessary precautions on the job not to endanger yourself or others
  • To make sure necessary safety equipment is used properly
  • To follow all procedures properly
  • To comply with the Canada Labour Code, Part II. Employees who require assistance should self-identify to their immediate supervisor
  • To report every workplace accidents or other occurrence
Employer responsibilities
  • To provide every person granted access to the workplace by the employer with prescribed safety materials, equipment, devices and clothing
  • To make sure necessary safety equipment is provided, used properly, and maintained
  • To inform employees and supervisors of any hazards and how to handle them
  • To ensure that procedures are followed in the workplace
  • To provide, in the prescribed manner, each employee with the information, instruction, training and supervision necessary to respond to workplace emergencies and to ensure their health and safety at work
  • To investigate all workplace hazards, incidents, injuries or illnesses
  • To report any workplace hazards, incidents or injuries to health and safety committee members
Managers/supervisors must
  • Exercise due diligence to eliminate or minimize any hazards
  • Comply with the Canada Labour Code, Part II and the standards set out in the Canada Occupational Health and Safety Regulations, Regulation 19, and TBS’s Policy on Occupational Safety and Health and related standards
  • Ensure employees are aware of the violence prevention program

Each federal institution should have its own Occupational Health and Safety Policy, in accordance with the Canada Labour Code, Part II. This federal legislation is intended to prevent workplace-related accidents, injuries and illnesses, and to promote a healthy and safe workplace.

All employees should familiarize themselves with their respective departmental policies.
