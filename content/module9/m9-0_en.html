Introduction and Objectives

The following audio is a duplication of the on-screen text:

Welcome to Module 10 – Safety and Security of Employees

Employees have a right to a workplace that is safe, healthy and violence-free; this includes working on government premises, and working off site. They need to be protected against hazardous situations, threats or vulnerabilities, and be informed of what actions they should take to protect their safety, both in the workplace and while on travel status.

Employees also have a responsibility to take care of their own health and safety, and that of other persons who may be affected by their acts or omissions.

Objectives

Upon completion of this session, you will be able to:

  • recognize various workplace situations or hazards that might affect workplace safety
  • list various threats or vulnerabilities
  • recognize best practices to protect yourself and safeguard government interests
  • respond to a fire, lockdown or shelter-in-place event

It should be noted that this lesson focuses specifically on situations, hazards, threats, vulnerabilities and practices that are within the scope of the Policy on Government Security, i.e. those related to prevention of violence in the workplace. Additional information on health and safety related obligations in these situations is outlined in Preparing for emergencies and evacuation of buildings: A guide for federal departments.
