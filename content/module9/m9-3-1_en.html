Drill Requirements

As per the BEET directive, every building occupied by at least 20 federal government employees must have a Building Emergency and Evacuation Team that will conduct at least 1 emergency evacuation drill and at least 1 lockdown or shelter‑in‑place exercise each year for all federal employees. 

Emergency Evacuation

A method of emptying a building quickly of personnel in the event of a fire or other emergency.  In most cases, the building's existing fire alarm system is activated and the building is evacuated.

Lockdown

A situation in which employees stop work and gather in a room that can be locked or in another safe area and await instructions from building officials or law enforcement officials. To be used only when there is a major incident or threat of violence within the building or in relation to the building.

Shelter-in-place

A situation when it is desirable to secure the building because of a situation outside and not related to the building (whether it is a criminal activity, such as a bank robbery nearby, or an environmental or weather-related situation such as a chemical spill or extreme weather conditions). Occupants are asked to remain where they are until further instructions are provided.

While an armed-intruder incident is highly unlikely, these attacks are becoming more common around the world, and federal government employees in Canada must be prepared to act if such an event occurs. As such, federal organizations should deliver staff information programs for armed-intruder incidents and conduct annual lockdown or shelter-in-place drills.

If ever you are faced with an armed-intruder incident, there are 3 things you can do that can help make a difference: RUN, HIDE, or DEFEND. It is important to note that these are options, NOT a series of steps; your options may change depending on the circumstances.

Visit the Armed Intruder webpage on the School’s online learning platform (GCcampus) to learn more about how to prepare for such an event and to watch the Government of Canada training video, “Armed Intruder: Do You Know What To Do?”

 

To learn more about the best strategies for responding to an armed intruder situation, contact your departmental security office.
