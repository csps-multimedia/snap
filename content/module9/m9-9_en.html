What is an EAP?

The Employee Assistance Program (EAP) is a confidential, short-term counseling, assessment and referral program, which is offered on a voluntary basis through Health Canada's Employee Assistance Services* and is free for employees, their spouses and dependents.

*Please verify with your own department or your required training coordinator, as some departments may have other programs in place.

What is the purpose of an EAP?

An EAP offers help with the resolution of problems that are affecting you and your work performance. These problems, however, do not have to be caused by workplace issues. Employee assistance programs are designed to help people understand or overcome all kinds of personal problems.

What types of services does an EAP offer?

EAP providers typically manage the following issues:

  • personal
  • job
  • relationship
  • eldercare, childcare, parenting
  • harassment
  • substance abuse
  • separation and loss
  • balancing work and family
  • financial or legal
  • family violence
