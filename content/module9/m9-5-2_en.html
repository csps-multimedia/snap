What Do I Do?

  • Be on the lookout for individuals who use information-gathering techniques to obtain your personal information.
  • Protect your information online—never post personal information to social networking sites or in e‑mails.
  • Only allow friends you know to access your social networking sites, blogs, Twitter feeds, and other sites.
  • Don’t open suspicious e‑mail attachments, click on unknown links, or download any programs in response to an e‑mail.
  • Do not validate personal information online.
  • Ensure your web browser has the latest security features, and that your system uses updated firewall, antivirus, and anti-spyware software.
  • Avoid storing your credit card numbers or Social Insurance Number on your computer.
  • Use strong passwords and do not allow sites to “remember” them.
  • Shred or destroy items with your name and address, such as preapproved credit card offers, insurance and loan applications, bills, and credit card receipts. Don’t discard them in your recycling or waste bins.
  • Don’t give out credit card numbers or other personal information over the phone unless it’s to a trusted person or you initiated the call yourself.

Learn more in Module 7 — Information Technology Security. You can also find a lot of great information about cyber security on the GETCYBERSAFE.CA webpage!

Techniques to obtain your personal information

Examples of techniques to obtain your personal information include:

  • shoulder surfing
  • dumpster diving
  • data mining
  • social engineering
  • phishing
  • spear phishing
