To practice the basic duties related to a screening request and to experience the process to assess a person’s reliability as well as their loyalty to Canada.

As a screening analyst, you have to be able to monitor forms, be familiar with the requirements of a reliability status or a Secret or Top Secret security clearance and do sufficient follow‑ups with the candidates. You have to be creative to get information that is sometimes difficult to obtain, use critical thinking and your communication skills, network and demonstrate the ability to analyze and synthesize throughout the screening process.

Practicing with this course will allow you to work with more self-confidence.

This course includes three case studies and all the tools needed to analyze the requests (one for a reliability status, one for a Secret security clearance and one for a Top Secret security clearance). The three studies are inspired by real cases.

A classroom session during which you can go into greater depth with the interview, a critical step of the screening process, will follow the course.

You can access the necessary documents for each case and the tools in two ways: by clicking the link found in the text or by selecting the tabs marked Documents or Tools. Consult the help section to better understand the documents and tools available to you.
