Exercise 2

Selecting the right security screening

Beth needs to perform security screenings for 3 employees but she needs help in deciding what she should do. It is extremely important that she get it right because all casual as well as indeterminate employees, students and contract workers with access to government information or assets must undergo a screening process.

Activity

Please help Beth determine which type of security screening she should choose, based on the information provided about each case.

Jack is a casual employee who will be at the department for a period of 6 months. He finished his first year of university, and then decided that he wanted to take a break from his studies to gain some valuable work experience.

Jack will be performing general administrative tasks. He will only have access to the departmental computer network and to Protected A and B information.

What type of security screening does Beth need to do for Jack?

Your answer is correct!

Beth correctly initiates a reliability status screening for Jack. Any employee with access to protected information or assets, regardless of the duration of an assignment, appointment or contract, need reliability status.

Your answer is incorrect.

Beth initiates a reliability status and security clearance.

Beth should have chosen to perform a reliability status screening, which is the type of security screening needed for any employee with access to protected information and assets, regardless of the duration of an assignment, appointment or contract.


Mary has been working at Department X for the last 5 years. She currently holds a reliability status and has recently won a competition as the manager of a small team. In her new role Mary will be required to work with classified information and assets up to the Secret level.

What action does Beth need to take?

Your answer is correct!

Beth must verify that Mary’s reliability status is still valid, confirm the security requirements of Mary’s position and immediately initiate the process to obtain her Secret security clearance before she can be appointed to the position.

This is the appropriate level of security in this case, given that Mary needs to handle information classified up to and including the Secret level.


Your answer is incorrect.

Beth verifies that Mary’s reliability status is still valid and decides incorrectly that this is all Mary needs. Had Beth verified the security requirements of Mary’s position, she would have realized that a security clearance was needed.


Bob has been hired through an external competition process. His new department needed lawyers with private-sector experience to handle a new federal program targeting the financial industry.

In his day-to-day work, Bob will need to liaise with the minister’s office and have access to top secret information.

What types of security screening does Beth need to do for Bob?

Your answer is correct!

Given that Bob is a new employee in the federal public service and needs access to very sensitive information, Beth will do both a reliability check and a security clearance at the Top-Secret level.

Your answer is incorrect.

Given the position requires access to Top-Secret information, Bob would require both a reliability status and security clearance.
