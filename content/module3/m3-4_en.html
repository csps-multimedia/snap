Summary – Best Practices

The following audio is a duplication of the on-screen text:

The purpose of security screening is to determine the honesty, trustworthiness, reliability, and loyalty of individuals to Canada who will have access to government information and assets (both Protected and Classified).

A level of security screening is assigned to a position and based on the duties performed and the sensitivity of information to which an individual may have access.

A security briefing formalizes the granting of a security screening level and the individual’s agreement to safeguard government information and assets in accordance with security requirements detailed in government and departmental security policies and standards.

Like official languages, security screening needs to be updated periodically. It may also be needed if there is a change in position requirements.

A decision to deny or revoke a security screening level would mean that an individual would no longer meet the conditions of employment and their employment would be negatively impacted. In such cases a review for cause must be conducted.

Individuals must be given an opportunity to explain adverse information before a decision to deny or revoke is reached and must be informed in writing of their rights of access to review or redress mechanisms.
