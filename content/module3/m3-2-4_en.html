Key Points

  • A valid security screening is a condition of employment and must be satisfied prior to the person being employed within the federal government.
  • Managers must ensure that employees who have not been granted the required type and level of security screening are not given access to protected or classified information.
  • New duties or a change in position may require an individual to have a higher level of screening (upgrade). Individuals will be required to reconfirm their consent agreeing to the necessary verifications of their personal information before the screening process begins.
  • If an individual without the appropriate clearance inadvertently has access to information for which they have not been granted the appropriate type and level of security screening, they should notify their manager.
  • By signing the security briefing form, individuals acknowledge that they have been granted a security screening level and formally agree to abide by the security policy and to apply security safeguards identified in various policies and standards.
  • If a reliability status or security clearance is either denied or revoked, the individual cannot be assigned to the position for which the security screening action was completed.
