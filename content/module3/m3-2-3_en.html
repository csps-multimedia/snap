Denials and Revocations

As a result of a security screening process, a reliability status or security clearance may be denied or revoked if:

  • information required to render a favourable decision is unavailable
  • adverse information is deemed to represent a risk
  • an individual has deliberately provided false information

Prior to arriving at a decision, a review for cause will be conducted, whereby security officials are expected to provide a fair and objective assessment. Individuals must be given an opportunity to explain adverse information before a decision is reached. They must also be informed in writing of their rights of access to review or redress mechanisms where a decision to deny or revoke reliability status or a security clearance has been reached.

An individual may have his security clearance denied or revoked if he is deemed to be a threat to the security of Canada.

Since a reliability status or security clearance is a condition of employment, a denial or revocation could severely impact the future employability of the individual within the federal government.
