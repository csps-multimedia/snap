Security with Non-Governmental Organizations Outside of a Contractual Agreement

The Government of Canadaâ€™s Policy on Government Security requires that all individuals who will have access to government information and assets are security screened at the appropriate level prior to being provided such access. This includes situations such as sharing sensitive information or assets with universities or research institutions, municipal/provincial governments, or other non-federal government organizations.

The Policy on Government Security requires that government information, assets and facilities entrusted to or shared with organizations outside the government of Canada are afforded an appropriate level of protection throughout their life cycle.

Note:

Consult with Legal Services on the best approach to take given the particularities of any initiative. In some cases a Memorandum of Understanding may be necessary, while in other cases a formal Exchange of Letters may suffice. The important thing to remember is that the non-federal governmental organizations MUST be advised in writing about the security requirements of the exchange, and they must agree to comply with those requirements BEFORE any exchange of sensitive information or asset takes place.
