Welcome to Module 8 - Security in Contracts and Other Arrangements

This module presents an overview of the importance of security in business processes involving parties outside the federal government (e.g. such as contracting with the private sector or research initiatives with academia).

Policy on Government Security (Appendix A): Security requirements associated with contracts and other arrangements must be identified and documented, and related security controls must be implemented and monitored throughout all stages of the contracting or arrangement process to provide reasonable assurance that information, individuals, assets and services associated with the contract or arrangement are adequately protected.

When federal-government functions or services are performed under contract by third parties, care must be taken to ensure that the government continues to fulfil its privacy obligations. The personal information must be managed so that the government institution conforms to the fair information practices embodied in the Privacy Act (sections 4 thru 8), the Privacy Regulations, and TBS's Policy on Privacy Protection, as well as any other privacy policy instruments that apply.

Guidance Document—Taking Privacy into Account before Making Contracting Decisions: This document is intended to provide advice to federal-government institutions whenever they consider contracting out activities in which Canadians’ personal information is handled or accessed by private-sector agencies under contract.

Objectives

Upon completion of this session, you will be able to:

Identify the policies and requirements for implementing proper security practices in business processes, such as contracting and information sharing agreements, with non-federal governmental organizations.
