Security and Contract Management Requirements

Before an organization can be awarded a contract, security screenings are required to ensure that the individuals involved in the contract have the required security screening level (reliability status or security clearance) and that the organization has the capacity to safeguard the information and assets that may be shared under the contract.

Additionally, for those contracted individuals who will not have access to Protected or Classified information, but will work within a sensitive area, they must be site access screened.

Screening can be conducted by the departments for contracts within the department's delegated contracting responsibilities. Departments should consider using the services of Public Services and Procurement Canada (PSPC) when the security requirements are complex. For international security contracts, departments must always go through PSPC.

Depending on the type of asset involved, there are two types of organizational security screenings:

Designated Organization Screening

An administrative determination that an organization is eligible, from a security viewpoint, to access Protected information and assets.

A Designated Organization Screening is based on an assessment of the following elements:

  • the completion of reliability status screening
  • a review of the security measures for the care and custody of Protected information and assets
Facility Security Clearance

An administrative determination that an organization is eligible, from a security viewpoint to access Classified information and assets.

Facility Security Clearance is based on an assessment of the following elements:

  • the organization is not under adverse foreign influence
  • the owners, officers, board of directors, executives and partners of the organization can be trusted to participate in Classified contracts
  • the organization can protect Classified information or assets at its facility

There are different types of Designated Organization Screenings and Facility Security Clearances, depending on the security requirements of the contract. For further details, contact your departmental contracting officer.

Screenings and clearances can be suspended or revoked if an organization fails to maintain required security standards.
