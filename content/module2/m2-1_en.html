Definitions

We should begin by defining some commonly used terms, as follows:

Threat – A threat is any potential event or act that could cause one or more of the following to occur: harm, injury, unauthorized disclosure, destruction, removal, modification or interruption of sensitive information, assets or services.

Risk – The potential that a given threat will cause an identified injury. Risk is often expressed as a combination of the likelihood and impact of an event.

Compromise – The unauthorized access to, disclosure, destruction, removal, modification, use or interruption of assets or information.

Vulnerability – An inadequacy related to security that could increase susceptibility to compromise or injury.
