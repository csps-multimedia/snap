The Classification Program

The Policy on Classification defines the Classification Program as the infrastructure for the effective management and control of the classification of positions in the Core Public Administration (CPA). Its key components include policies, directives, classification standards, occupational group definitions, job descriptions, job evaluation standards, active monitoring, and grievance mechanisms.

The goal of the Classification program is to establish the relative value of all work in the core public administration in an equitable, consistent, efficient and effective manner, and to provide a basis for the compensation of public service employees.

To this end, the Classification program defines the occupational groups and levels using job evaluation standards to assign a relative value to the work done by the positions.

The discipline practised by the Classification community is often called “organization and classification” (OC) because, in addition to evaluating (classifying) positions, it also involves a review and modelling of an organization based on a set of parameters (mandate, business objectives, budget, resources, etc.), in order to develop the optimum organizational structure that will enable the manager or the organization to deliver its mandate.

Organization

In the context of the CPA, organization refers to the alignment of an organization’s activities with the roles and responsibilities mandated by Parliament and reflecting government priorities. It is achieved through a process of review, research and modelling, called organizational design, to help managers establish an optimal structure to meet defined objectives and mandates.

Classification

Classification is a process by which the relative value of work in the CPA is determined, resulting in an appropriate occupational group and level. This is done by analyzing the content of the job description, the organizational context and relativity, while adhering to the requirements of the Treasury Board Policy on Classification and related instruments.

Classification supports the development of coherent organizational structures and work relationships and forms the framework for effective resourcing, labour relations, training, performance management, and effective management, planning and use of human resources within an organization.
