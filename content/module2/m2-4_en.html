Exercise 1

Identifying Types of Threats

The following statements are based on common situations you may encounter in your day-to-day work.

Read each one carefully and identify whether the situation described poses a deliberate threat, an accidental threat or a natural hazard.

Activity

Answer the following questions using what you have learned in this module.

Your department’s Webmaster has just sent out an e-mail informing all employees that the departmental website is down following a vicious hacker attack. According to the message, the hackers were unsuccessful in their attempt to gain access to sensitive financial information and chose to deface the department’s public website instead.

Your answer is correct!

This is a deliberate threat caused by an individual seeking to gain access to sensitive information.

Individual threats may also come from insiders who are seeking personal gain, reacting to a perceived injustice such as being laid off, or have been bribed or coerced into wrongdoing.

Your answer is incorrect.

This is a deliberate threat caused by an individual seeking to gain access to sensitive information.

Individual threats may also come from insiders who are seeking personal gain, reacting to a perceived injustice such as being laid off, or have been bribed or coerced into wrongdoing.


The aide of a high-level executive has taken a cab to go to an important meeting. After being caught in traffic for over half an hour, he decides to exit the cab and walk to his meeting instead. In his haste, he leaves behind a USB key containing sensitive information. This information is later leaked to the press.

Your answer is correct!

This is an accidental threat.  The accidental loss of laptops, mobile devices and portable storage devices such as USB drives is very likely to lead to disclosures of classified and personal information.

Your answer is incorrect.

This is an accidental threat.  The accidental loss of laptops, mobile devices and portable storage devices such as USB drives is very likely to lead to disclosures of classified and personal information.


Meteorological Service of Canada has reported that a massive ice storm is going to affect the National Capital Region. As a result, facility managers are taking the necessary steps to minimize damage to buildings and other physical assets, and ensure the safety of employees. All managers are verifying that plans and measures are in place to ensure the continuity of essential operations and services.

Your answer is correct!

Ice storms, floods and extreme weather are all natural hazards.

Other examples of natural hazards include geological hazards such as earthquakes and tsunamis and disease such as SARS and H1N1 epidemics/pandemics.

Your answer is incorrect.

Ice storms, floods and extreme weather are all natural hazards.

Other examples of natural hazards include geological hazards such as earthquakes and tsunamis and disease such as SARS and H1N1 epidemics/pandemics.


In 2003, a widespread electrical outage affected an estimated 10 million people in Ontario and disrupted government operations. The blackout was caused by a software bug in the alarm system and it cascaded into a widespread outage on the electrical grid through a combination of operator errors.

Your answer is correct!

Software bugs are an important source of accidental threats.

They can lead to system outages which, when compounded by human error, can significantly disrupt the delivery of services.

Your answer is incorrect!

Software bugs are an important source of accidental threats.

They can lead to system outages which, when compounded by human error, can significantly disrupt the delivery of services.

Your answer is correct!

Deliberate threats can be caused by groups in their attempt to protest government policies or engage in civil disobedience.

Other deliberate threats may come from groups such as organized crime, who may attempt to gain access to personal and law enforcement information, or foreign intelligence services, who may seek to recruit insiders or to gain access to national security or classified information.
