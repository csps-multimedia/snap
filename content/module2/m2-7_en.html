Exercise 2

Is this a sign of an insider threat?

Given that insiders are very difficult to detect, it is important to recognize the signs of an insider at work.

Activity

Using the following questions determine if the characters involved are behaving in a way that points to the work of an insider or if their behaviour is normal.

Alan is a consultant hired to advise senior management on human resources reorganization.

In the last few days you have noticed that Alan accesses your department’s financial administration system, without authorization, on a regular basis and has printed several monthly reports. These reports contain sensitive information which could be used by a company to gain an unfair advantage over competitors.

Your answer is correct!

This is a possible sign of an insider threat.

When someone is accessing and/or modifying systems and printing sensitive documents outside of their job function it is a sign of an insider threat.

You should notify your manager and/or departmental security officer (DSO) immediately.

Your answer is incorrect.

Denise has been complaining of not having been recognized or promoted despite her significant contribution to the success of important projects in her department. Recently she has started to wear expensive clothes and flashy jewellery and is constantly taking long lunches.

Your answer is correct!

You may be in the presence of an insider threat if a person engages in negative discussions about not receiving the recognition or promotion they deserve, changes their personal habits and/or adopts unusual routines (wearing flashy clothes when they usually do not, leaving early from work, long lunches).

You should notify your manager and/or departmental security office immediately.

Your answer is incorrect.

You may be in the presence of an insider threat if a person engages in negative discussions about not receiving the recognition or promotion they deserve, changes their personal habits and/or adopts unusual routines (wearing flashy clothes when they usually do not, leaving early from work, long lunches).

You should notify your manager and/or departmental security office immediately.


Nathalie is very technically savvy and likes helping her colleagues with their computer problems. She often installs non-business applications on her corporate workstation and has done the same on others. Lately, she seems to arrive earlier than everyone else and leaves late in the evening. She seems to spend a lot of time on the Internet.

Your answer is correct!

You may be in the presence of an insider threat if someone is installing non-business application software on corporate systems, working unusual hours (early, late, weekends or holidays) and engaging in unusual activity on unmonitored e-mail and/or the Internet.

You should notify your manager and/or departmental security officer (DSO) immediately.

Your answer is incorrect.

You may be in the presence of an insider threat if someone is installing non-business application software on corporate systems, working unusual hours (early, late, weekends or holidays) and engaging in unusual activity on unmonitored e-mail and/or the Internet.

You should notify your manager and/or departmental security officer (DSO) immediately.


Elaine has been working for her department for the last 10 years. She always arrives on time, delivers outstanding products, and is willing to take on more responsibilities.

Last month, Elaine’s mother was diagnosed with a terminal disease and Elaine became her caregiver. Since then, Elaine has started to arrive late and she is unable to take care of her usual workload.

Your answer is correct!

This is NOT a sign of an insider threat.

Although Elaine is having some personal issues, they are not uncharacteristic for the type of situation she is experiencing at home.

However, if you notice new personal or interpersonal issues that are unjustified and uncharacteristic, it is advisable to pay attention and to notify your manager and/or departmental security officer (DSO) if the situation escalates.
