Web 2.0 and Social Media

The Policy on Acceptable Network and Device Use establishes the expected behaviours of users. Employees should know about these behaviours to help protect against potential security or privacy breaches.

Open access to the Internet, including external Web 2.0 tools and services, introduces inherent security risks. Use of these technologies must be done in accordance with the Policy on Government Security, the Policy on Acceptable Network and Device Use, and your respective departmental policies.

Social media websites and tools are a way for people to connect and share information with each other, and include blogs, wikis, and forums such as Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, Google+ and Wikipedia.

Social media websites may be used for official purposes, professional development or limited personal use in accordance with acceptable use policies. Only those individuals who have been authorized to represent the department can use social media accounts for official purposes. Some departments may impose further restrictions on their use. Check with your departmental security office or IT security services before using social media.

These tools present many opportunities for more efficient service delivery, faster knowledge and information sharing, and more effective collaboration and Open Government. However, they can also pose a threat as departments and their networks are frequently targeted by criminals looking to gather information.

  2. Ensure that any business information posted has been approved for release
  3. Use discretion when posting job-related information
  4. Avoid using specific identification of your job role, such as title or location
  5. You should not publish your official government e-mail address on the Internet because cyber threats are able to use them for spam or other malicious purpose
  6. Be careful when accessing unknown website links or attachments
  7. Report any suspected security incidents

Be cautious when posting your own personal information on social media platforms for both privacy and cyber-security reasons. Cyber threats are actively gathering personal information to perform social engineering attacks, to assume an identity or to gain unauthorized access to online applications or services. Remember that once information is posted, you no longer have control over it, and it will remain on the Internet for a long time.

Web 2.0 tools and services

Includes Internet-based tools and services that allow for participatory multi-way information sharing, dialogue, syndication, and user-generated content. This can include social media and collaborative technologies.
