Passwords

Passwords provide a simple, direct means of protecting a system or account. Using proper passwords is the first line of defense against intruders.

Passwords are generally used in combination with some form of identification, such as a user name, account number or e-mail address. The user name establishes the identity of the user. The password is a word, a phrase or combination of miscellaneous characters which is known only to the user. The password authenticates the identity of the user.

You are accountable for all actions taken under your user name and password: this is your electronic identity. Never share your password with anyone!

Be sure to use different passwords for different accounts. For example, never use the same passwords for your work or bank accounts as your personal accounts like Facebook, YouTube or Twitter.

While passwords are a vital component of system security, they can be cracked or broken relatively easily. You need to select proper passwords in accordance with departmental password policies.


Tips for Creating Passwords

Tips for Creating Passwords

Below are some tips for creating an appropriate password:

  • Use a minimum of 8 to 10 characters that combine letters, numbers, and special characters (e.g. #, $, &, etc.)
  • Do not use personal information such as the names of your children, address, etc;
  • Do not use dictionary words or common phrases
  • If you do write it down, always store it in a secure location such as a locked cabinet or safe (NOT under your keyboard or phone)
  • Change passwords frequently and avoid replacing just a number
  • You never need to tell anyone your password, including IT support staff. If for any reason you believe your password has been compromised, change it immediately and report the incident
  • When an account is newly created for you, you will be given an initial default password: always change it as soon as possible
  • For more guidance on how to establish best practices to securely manage passwords in the Government of Canada, visit the Password Guidance webpage on Canada.ca

Note:

ALWAYS lock your workstation every time you leave. Use screensavers and timeouts to lock your computer when you inadvertently leave your workstation unattended.

Remember:

Protect your password! YOU could be held responsible for all activities that occur under your user name and log-in credentials.
