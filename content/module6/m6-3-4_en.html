Use of Encryption

The purpose of encrypting a storage device, file or e-mail is to ensure that it cannot be viewed by anyone other than the intended recipients.

Approved encryption must be used to send sensitive information by e-mail and to store government information on portable storage devices.

MyKey is the government standard for the purposes of encrypting and decrypting files, e-mails, and various self-service online GC tools, such as accessing your pay information via the Compensation Web Access (CWA) website. MyKey is approved to secure information up to Protected B.

Your department will also provide encryption tools to protect portable storage devices. Your departmental security office or IT security services can provide additional guidance.

Key Message

Do not send sensitive information by e-mail unless it has been properly encrypted.

Only use approved systems for processing, storing or transmitting Protected C and Classified information. Contact your IT security services for additional guidance.
