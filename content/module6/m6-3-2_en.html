Social Engineering

Social engineering refers to manipulating people into performing actions or divulging confidential information. The attacker attempts to trick an individual for the purpose of information gathering, fraud or system access. Social engineering is often one of many steps in a more complex scheme to gain access to electronic resources, data or personal information. It is often associated with identity theft, fraudulent activities, cybercrime or espionage.

Never disclose personal information unless you are certain that the requesting individual is not an imposter and that he/she has a legitimate need to know for business purposes.

Examples

Examples

  • Using an invented scenario (an elaborate lie) to engage a targeted victim in a manner that increases the chance the victim will divulge information or perform actions that would be unlikely in ordinary circumstances.
  • Attempting to get individuals to disclose information about their various financial accounts over the phone or by e-mail.
  • Impersonation: Passing themselves off, over the phone or by e-mail, as employees or some other person you would normally trust, and requesting personal or corporate information from you. There have been cyber attacks using external e-mails (e.g. from Gmail or Hotmail) that appear to come from a manager.
