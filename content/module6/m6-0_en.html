Welcome to Module 7 - Information Technology Security

Do you use a computer, the Internet, e-mail, GCpedia, Twitter, LinkedIn, or other social networking sites or blogs? If so, do you understand the risks?

Information Technology (IT) is widely incorporated into our day-to-day activities. We are a digital society; we play, learn, socialize, communicate, and do business electronically. Electronic systems are the preferred means of creating, using, and managing information.

All public servants are responsible for protecting the information stored on our computers, laptops, portable media storage devices, mobile devices and networks – whether at home, at work, or while travelling – in accordance with the Policy on Government Security.

Objectives

Upon completion of this session, you will be able to:

  • Explain the most important IT security requirements
  • Review your responsibilities in protecting government systems, devices, and the information you hold
  • Summarize what is considered acceptable use of government networks and devices, the Internet, and social media
