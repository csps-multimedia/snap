What Is Information Technology Security?

Information Technology (IT) security encompasses a number of security safeguards to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of electronically stored, processed or transmitted information, as well as the IT devices entrusted to us.

As employees, we encounter security threats on a daily basis, especially if we use the Internet. As discussed in Module 3, threats can be deliberate, accidental or a result of natural hazards. Users should be particularly concerned about deliberate cyber threats and human errors. Be aware that the desktop computer is a primary target for cyber attacks. In short, your actions can significantly increase the risk to the Government of Canada.

By being aware of potential threats and vulnerabilities and by following sound security practices, you will be able to protect yourself, your workplace and government information.

IT devices

Tools used to gather, process, receive, display, transmit, reconfigure, scan, store or destroy information electronically.

Cyber attacks

A cyber attack is deliberate exploitation of computer systems and networks. Cyber attacks use various means of malicious acts that either steals, alters, or destroys information.
