Use of Portable Media Storage Devices

Our increasing mobility also means a greater reliance on portable technology to get work done. Portable media devices that offer storage or memory into which users can store information are considered portable media storage devices. Only portable data storage devices issued by departments/​agencies are authorized to be used to store GC information.

Examples of portable data storage devices include:

  • USB devices (e.g. memory sticks, external hard drives)
  • eSATA (External Serial Advanced Technology Attachment) devices
  • tablets, laptops, smart devices (e.g. BlackBerry), and cameras
  • portable media—tapes, optical discs (e.g. CDs and DVDs)

Risks of Using Portable Storage Devices

As the booming popularity of these devices continues to rise, so do the potential risks to the government. The improper use and handling of portable storage devices or the improper storage of government information on these devices can pose significant risks to the security of government information, and may violate policies for security, privacy protection and information management.

These risks include:

  1. The unauthorized access or use of information stored on the device
  2. The introduction of malicious software onto government IT networks
  3. The loss or theft of the device, and the information it contains

Appropriate Security Safeguards while Using Portable Storage Devices

It is important that appropriate security safeguards be taken. A recently published policy implementation notice on the Secure Use of Portable Storage Devices introduces a number of stringent security requirements. Departments and agencies will train employees on the proper use of portable storage devices and the associated security tools. Users will be required to sign a portable storage device user agreement.

Some of the security requirements for portable storage devices are as follows:

  • Portable storage devices must be physically secured (not attached to your lanyard), at all times, as appropriate to the highest level of security classification of the information stored on it
  • Portable storage devices must be labelled to indicate the highest classification level of information that has been stored on the device
  • When transporting a portable storage device with government information stored on it, you must keep it under your constant control and possession at all times
  • Employees must only use devices that have been approved and issued by their department for storage of government information
  • Portable storage devices are intended for the temporary storage of information only and must not be used as permanent document repositories
  • All government information stored on portable storage devices must be encrypted

When travelling abroad, it is critical that users be particularly vigilant in exercising recommended security controls. Those who have potentially sensitive information on their devices may want to consider having them specially configured for travel to minimize the risks and potential access to their sensitive information. Contact your departmental security office or IT security services for more information.
