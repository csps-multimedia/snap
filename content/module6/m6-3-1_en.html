Malicious Software and Symptoms

Malware, short for malicious software, is software used by cyber attackers to disrupt computer operations, gather sensitive information or gain unauthorized access to computer systems.

Malware comes in a wide variety of forms, including computer viruses, worms, Trojan horses, rootkits, keystroke loggers, spyware, adware, and other malicious programs. Malicious software can steal passwords, files or other confidential data, modify files or provide a backdoor allowing unauthorized access. Some malware can remain undetected for prolonged periods of time, stealing large volumes of sensitive information.

Malicious software can enter our systems through a variety of means, including infected e-mail and attachments, USB drives, and by browsing infected websites. Although software such as antivirus software and firewalls help to prevent the spread of malware on the network, malware is becoming increasingly sophisticated and can avoid detection. Users therefore need to take precautions to prevent malicious software from infecting our networks through social engineering, suspicious e-mails, untrustworthy websites, and weak passwords.

Symptoms of an Infected Workstation

The following may be symptoms that your workstation has been infected:

  • The system slows down
  • Unusual error messages
  • Unusual messages appear as pop-ups (greetings, insults, etc.)
  • Data loss
  • Frequent program crashes or the system or applications refusing to start
  • Countless e-mails coming or going

If you experience any of the above incidents, immediately report your suspicions to your help desk or departmental IT security staff.

Viruses

A computer virus is a type of malware that, when executed, replicates itself into other computer programs or data files, and performs some type of harmful activity on infected hosts.

Worms

A computer worm is a standalone malware computer program that replicates itself in order to spread to other computers. Unlike a computer virus, it does not need to attach itself to an existing program. Worms almost always cause at least some harm to the network.

Trojan horses

A Trojan horse is a type of malware that is disguised within a legitimate program while appearing to perform a desirable function. They tend to be invisible to average users, but often include a backdoor allowing unauthorized access to the target's computer to steal information or harm their host computer systems.

Rootkits

A rootkit is a stealthy type of malware designed to hide the existence of certain processes or programs from normal methods of detection and enable continued privileged access to a computer.

Keystroke loggers

Keystroke loggers is malware that records (or logs) the keys struck on a keyboard in a covert manner so that the person using the keyboard is unaware that their actions are being monitored (e.g. captures usernames and passwords).

Antivirus

Antivirus (AV) software is specialized security software that detects known forms of malware. They are not limited to viruses, but may be ineffective against some forms such as rootkits.

Firewalls

Firewall is specialized security software or device that blocks or restricts access to a computer or network.
