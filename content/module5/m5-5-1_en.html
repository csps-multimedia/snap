Clean-Desk Guidelines

Maintaining a clean work area is an important best practice in ensuring information management security. A clean desk will help reduce the risk of information theft, fraud or disclosure caused by sensitive information being left unattended.

At a minimum, employees should clear their desks at the end of each work day to prevent accidental loss of sensitive information.

Please consult the job aid – Clean-Desk Guidelines for some tips and best practices on how to keep your work area clean.

Clean-Desk Guidelines

Clean-Desk Tips:

Throughout the day:

  • Physically secure laptop computers with locking cables
  • Secure information on your computer by logging out or locking your workstation using Ctrl-Alt-Del when you are away from your desk
  • Store sensitive documents and computer media (CD-ROMs, DVDs and USB keys and drives) in locked desks and cabinets
  • Secure personal valuables

At the end of the day:

  • Tidy up and secure sensitive material
  • Secure equipment (laptop computers, BlackBerry’s, cell phones, etc.)
  • Clear documents from printers and fax machines
  • Lock drawers, cabinets, and office doors
