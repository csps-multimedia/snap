Confidentiality, Privacy, Integrity and Availability of Information

While the primary focus of the previous sections was confidentiality of information, privacy, integrity and availability of information are also important.

Confidentiality

Refers to the need to protect information from unauthorized access or disclosure.

Privacy

Refers to the need to protect government-held personal information from unauthorized access or disclosure.

Integrity

Refers to the accuracy, completeness, and authenticity of information. Integrity and authenticity are important aspects from both an information management and security point of view. The integrity level is based on the potential injury that could result from unauthorized modification of information. A loss of integrity could lead to wrong decisions, liability, financial loss (e.g. modification of high-value financial transactions) or loss of public safety (e.g. tampering with critical health or water safety data).

Availability

Refers to the state of being accessible and usable in a timely and reliable manner. From a security point of view, availability is based on the potential injury that could result from unauthorized destruction or disruption of services. Interruptions to delivery of critical services could impact the health, safety, economic well-being, and security of Canadians. Interruptions to other important services could impact a department’s ability to achieve its objectives or services to Canadians.

Key Message

Continued availability of critical services is addressed through departments’ business continuity plans. You need to know if the work you do supports a critical service and, if so, you need to understand your role in your department’s business continuity plan. Normally, this information is provided to you upon employment and you will be briefed in your role.
