Need to Know Principle

Personnel are not entitled to access merely because it would be convenient for them to know or because of status, rank, office or level of clearance.

The department originating or acquiring sensitive information and assets is responsible for identifying any need to know constraints or limitations. In some cases distribution may be limited to a specific group of individuals with the need to know. More specific guidelines may be identified by some departments.

Key Message

Your level of security screening does not give you access to all documents in the associated category. You should only have access to information that you need to do your job. This is known as the “need to know” principle.
