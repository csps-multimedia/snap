Electronic Security

Consult with your manager or departmental security office to determine the highest level of sensitivity that can be processed or stored on your computer, network and associated storage devices.

Only approved systems that are not connected to the Internet should be used for processing Protected C and Classified information.

You should never store sensitive information on portable storage devices such as USB devices or cell phones unless they are properly encrypted. These devices can be easily lost, stolen, or misplaced. Sensitive information, including personal information, stored on unencrypted devices increases the risk of unauthorized access or disclosure. Consult with your manager or the departmental security office to determine your specific departmental procedures.

When using a network printer, do not leave sensitive information unattended. Use an approved local printer in a secure location for printing Protected C and Classified information.
