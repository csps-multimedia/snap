Marking Information

For Protected information, mark the upper right-hand corner of all the pages of the document in bold capital letters with “PROTECTED A”, “PROTECTED B” or “PROTECTED C”.

For Classified information, the cover or first page of a document should be clearly marked in bold capital letters as “TOP SECRET”, “SECRET” or “CONFIDENTIAL” to show the highest sensitivity level of the information in the document. Each page should be marked in the top right-hand corner with the highest sensitivity level of the information on the page.

Portable media storage devices should be marked to show the highest sensitivity level of the information.

In special cases, copies of documents need to be numbered in order to strictly control distribution.

Key Message

All paper and electronic documents – including emails – should be clearly marked.
