E-mail

E-mail can be easily intercepted and only approved secure networks should be used to send sensitive information.

Be careful not to inadvertently send sensitive information by eâ€‘mail that is not protected or to inadvertently send sensitive information to the wrong addressee.

Protected A & B information should be encrypted using a Government of Canada approved solution such as MyKey.

Only approved systems can be used for processing Protected C and Classified information. Stronger encryption that is approved by Communications Security Establishment Canada is required for transmitting Protected C and Classified information.

Key Messages

  • Never send an email containing sensitive information from your departmental network account to external accounts such as Gmail, Hotmail, etc. External email accounts are not secure and information can be intercepted and read by anyone.
  • Text and pin-to-pin messages should never be used for communicating sensitive information.
