Declassifying Information

The originator should recognize that once a security level is assigned, this determines the degree of protection required. It is not possible to reduce the level of protection without proper authorization unless the sensitivity level of the information is downgraded or declassified.

The originator is responsible for declassifying or downgrading the security level of information when it is appropriate. Originators of sensitive information should review its sensitivity on a periodic basis: further guidance may be obtained from your departmental security office. If the information is provided by another organization, the originating office should be consulted before declassifying or downgrading any information.
