Determining Security Levels

The Policy on Government Security provides guidance for determining whether information should be protected or classified. The Access to Information Act and the Privacy Act determine the right to access information, subject to exemptions and exclusions.

Key Message

In categorizing information, individuals must be careful not to over classify information because it is costly to protect higher security levels. It is up to the owner or originator of the information to assign the proper security levels.

Proper categorization of the sensitivity level (Protected or Classified) of the information requires an assessment of the degree of injury that would result if the information were disclosed.
