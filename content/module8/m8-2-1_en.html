How to Report a Security Event

You should report any security event to your manager or departmental security office immediately.

When reporting a security incident, you should provide as much information as possible including:

  • The date, time, and location the incident took place
  • Identify the information or assets that may have been affected
  • Personnel involved or other witnesses
  • Specific information related to the incident

You could be held accountable for failing to report an incident.

In case of privacy breaches, notify the departmental Access to Information and Privacy (ATIP) coordinator or the delegated authority for privacy as well as the departmental security office. Privacy breaches may also involve a breach of security: it is important to involve the ATIP coordinator or delegated authority and the departmental security office to ensure that the privacy of individuals and the security of assets are taken into account in the resolution process.

When reporting a privacy breach, you should provide as much information as possible including:

  • the nature and extent of the breach
  • the type of personal information involved
  • the parties involved
  • anticipated risks of harm
  • steps taken or to be taken to notify individuals
  • what remedial action has been taken
