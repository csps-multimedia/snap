Breaches

Security Breach

A security breach is an act or omission, deliberate or accidental, which results in an actual compromise. All breaches must be investigated and depending on the sensitivity of the information or assets involved, a breach can have serious consequences. This is challenging in relation to privacy breaches, as there can be a privacy breach without an actual compromise.

Privacy Breach

A privacy breach involves the improper or unauthorized creation, collection, use, disclosure, retention or disposition of personal information. “Personal information” means information about an identifiable individual that is recorded in any form. If the breach involves sensitive personal information and could reasonably be expected to cause injury or harm to the individual, it is considered “material.”

“Sensitive personal information” includes, but is not limited to:

  • medical, psychiatric or psychological information
  • information describing an individual’s finances such as income, assets, liabilities, net worth, bank balances, tax returns, financial history or activities, or creditworthiness
  • information concerning an individual’s racial or ethical origin, religious or political beliefs, associations or lifestyle

Key Message

You should be aware that security breaches can have lasting, costly and potentially harmful effects for the government and its employees. Privacy breaches that involve sensitive personal information can pose a risk of significant harm to the affected individuals. Therefore, you must be vigilant at all times and report breaches via the appropriate channels.

Examples of Security Events

Breaches:
  • unauthorized access to restricted-access areas, sensitive information or information technology systems
  • unauthorized disclosure of classified or protected information
  • uttering threats or actual events of workplace violence
  • unauthorized modification or deletion of information or computer system software
  • unauthorized disclosure or use of personal information (i.e. privacy breach)
  • any modification of a security measure (e.g. cabinet or safe) that reduces or impairs its function or effectiveness
  • unauthorized recording or other electronic devices within a restricted area

Serious security events and other events that pose a threat to health, life, property or environment, requires immediate action. Employees should be familiar with their department’s emergency procedures for the following kinds of events:

  • suspicious or unattended packages
  • threatening phone calls (particularly bomb threats)
  • serious violence (e.g. active shooter)
  • large, sudden demonstrations
